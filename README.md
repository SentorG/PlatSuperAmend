# Sentor_G | HeartGold VS Platinum Super Kaizo Comparison
A look at how the two Gen 4 games differ in relation to Super Kaizo IronMON, with suggestions for balancing.

## Comparison - Pros & Cons
| **HeartGold** | **Platinum** |
|:-|:-|
| 5 Potions and 3 visible items pre-Lab. Equippable. | Nothing pre-Lab. No crits for either side in Lab. |
| 1 hidden item post-Lab.  | 1 hidden & 1 visible item post-Lab. 1 Potion from an NPC. |
| First Trainer = Lv3, 6 VS our Lv7/8.<br>1 hidden afterwards and 2 visible items outside Dark Cave. | First Trainer = Lv8 VS our Lv6/7. Two more Lv8 Trainers. |
| **Violet City** has 1 hidden item and the Shard de Berry NPC. **Ruins of Alph** has 3 hidden and 4 Escape Rope items if you're lucky. | 2 hidden and 3 visible items once into **Jubilife City**. |
| **Sprout Tower:** Lv6-13 enter (depending on pivot/experience). Trainers have x9 Lv5s, x2 Lv9s, x4 Lv11s and a Lv15. x4 items. | **Trainer School:** Lv6-12 enter (depending on pivot/experience). Trainers have x2 Lv9s. x1 item and a Potion from the left-hand Trainer upon beating both. |
|  | Trainers post-School and pre-Gym 1 have Lv6-14s with an average level gap of 7 between our Pokémon and the enemy. 12 items and one Super Potion are picked up in this time. |
| **Gym 1:** Lv11, 11. Lv14. (Leader) x5 Lv14s and a Lv20. VS our Lv21/22 Pokémon. | **Gym 1:** Lv14, 14. Lv17. (Leader) x5 Lv18s and a Lv21. VS our Lv23/25 Pokémon. |
| <br><br>**TOTAL:**<br>5 Potions, 19 items (including Escape Rope checks).<br>High level gap VS our Pokémon.<br>Access to Shard de Berry NPC.<br><br> | <br><br>**TOTAL:**<br>1 Potion, 1 Super Potion and 20 items.<br>Low level gap VS our Pokémon.<br><br> |

### Up to Gym 1, both games seem mostly balanced- with the exception of the Lab battle where HeartGold is clearly much easier.

## Pivot Quality
| **HeartGold** | **Platinum** |
|:-|:-|
| 22 pivot options (including **Dark Cave**). | 31 pivot options (including **Route 203**). |
| Ability to take a Lv3 into the first Trainer. Can pivot onto another Pokémon and still be OK. | No chance at all taking a Lv3 into the first Trainer. Taking a Lv5 into the **Trainer School** is a huge risk. |
| Old Rod pivots after Gym 1 are a huge risk, but each of the 7 (overworld) fishing spots have 2 potential options. | Old Rod pivots are a huge factor for the Platinum pivot phase. 4 fishing spots open up once entered into **Jubilife City** and another cope spot on **Route 203**. The rates of the different levels are not too difficult to find if the runner is patient enough. With a maximum level of 23 on the Old Rod, this can be a lucrative play- but risky and heavily RNG-dependent. It must be noted that each fishing spot with the Old Rod only yields 1 encounter, unlike HeartGold's 2 per spot. |

Even though there are more pivot options in Platinum (and potentially giving you a better level advantage from fishing), shards can be found in item checks before access to Rock Smash. This gives a huge benefit to HeartGold (RNG depending) which Platinum does not have.

## After Gym 1
| **HeartGold** | **Platinum** |
|:-|:-|
| 7 rocks to smash, 1 hidden and 4 visible items. Trainers up to **Union Cave** have an average level gap of 11, with the exception of the fisherman with the Lv23. | 3 visible items. Trainers up to **Valley Windworks** have an average level gap of 9. Mars's ace is Lv26 and our Pokémon is typically Lv27/28. |
| **Union Cave** has 5 visible and 3 hidden items. The average level gap is 12. Getting into **Azalea Town**, 2 hidden and 1 visible item are available. | Leading up to **Eterna Forest**, there are 2 visible items. The average level gap is 10/12. |
| **Slowpoke Well:** Lv25/26 enter. Average level gap of 14. 1 visible and 1 hidden item. Rival 2 can be difficult with his Lv27 ace meeting our Lv28/29. | **Etnera Forest:** Lv30/31 enter. Average level gap of 12. 4 visible items and a mandatory tag-team battle with Cheryl. 2 more items can be picked up before Gym 2 and enough experience to get to Lv35/36. |
| **Gym 2:** Bugsy's Gym can be a big wall. Average level gap of 11. He leads with his Lv26 ace VS our Lv31/32 Pokémon. Upon beating Bugsy, **Ilex Forest** yields 3 hidden and 7 visible items. | **Gym 2:** Gardenia is considered one of the most difficult Gyms of all IronMON runs. Her Trainers have average level gap of 10 VS our Pokémon. Gardenia's team consists of x5 Lv30s and a Lv33 ace - all of which are forced fully evolved. For making it out alive, players are awarded 6 visible items. |
| <br><br>**TOTAL:**<br>28 item checks.<br>Similar, but higher level gap compared to Platinum.<br>Rocks could yield shards for extra healing/status items.<br><br> | <br><br>**TOTAL:**<br>17 item checks.<br>Close level gap VS our Pokémon + a fully evolved team-of-6 Gym Leader.<br><br> |

### This section of the game really illustrates how badly Platinum suffers for item checks.

Without the healing or status items to sustain the Pokémon through the tight level gap up to Gym 2, there's a very good chance you lose the run somewhere along here.
